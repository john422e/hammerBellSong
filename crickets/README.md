## Overview

*Phone Cricket* is a 3D-printable device designed for performance contexts, specifically for John Eagle's composition *"hammer, bell, song"* for mixed ensemble and electronics.

The object functions as a phone mount and performance interface element, integrating physical structure with sonic and performative use.

---

## Files Included

* 3D model files (`.stl`) for the Phone Cricket structure
* Phone mount components (adapted from GTPerrault's design; see attribution below)
* `LICENSE.txt` (Creative Commons Attribution-NonCommercial 4.0)

---

## Printing Instructions

* **Material:** PLA recommended
* **Color:** Black filament recommended
* **Scaling:** Print at **110%** for larger phones
* **Hardware:**

  * Use a **3/4" rubber washer** with the nut/threading in the phone mount and **1/2" rubber strip on the sides of the phone mount**
  *https://www.amazon.com/dp/B087C661MJ?ref=ppx_yo2ov_dt_b_fed_asin_title
  *https://www.amazon.com/dp/B0CLXMHPKR?ref=ppx_yo2ov_dt_b_fed_asin_title&th=1
  * Improves fit and stability

---

## Assembly Notes

* Assemble printed parts according to their geometric fit (top/bottom screw onto middle part)
* Insert phone mount components (threading through one of the holes in the middle part)
* Ensure washer is properly seated for secure phone attachment
